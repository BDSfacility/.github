## Biomedical Data Science Facility

The [Biomedical Data Science Facility](https://www.unige.ch/medecine/bdsf/en) (BDSF) at [Faculty of Medicine of UNIGE](https://www.unige.ch/medecine/en) supports research laboratories with bioinformatics and statistical expertise. We analyse various types of data, from omics to imaging, to create publication-ready visualizations and drive scientific discovery.\
Our goal is to advise, train, and assist scientists in making effective use of laboratory data across all disciplines of biomedical research.

### Overview of our activities

* **Consulting**: We offer comprehensive support, from project planning and experimental design to data analysis and interpretation of results. We produce high-quality figures accompanied by ad hoc statistics. We also assist in the drafting of scientific articles for the publication of results.
* **Analysis**: The BDSF's mission is to analyze, visualize, and interpret large-scale data, whether genomic, proteomic, lipidomic, cytometric, images, or clinical data such as questionnaires. We provide appropriate statistical analysis and also develop pipelines and visualization tools.
  * *OMICS*: scRNA-seq, scTCR-seq, Multiome, CITE-seq, Spatial transcriptomics, RNA-seq, BRB-seq, ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq...
  * *Cytometry*: Cytek Aurora, CyTOF
  * *Statistics*: Methodological advice, Quality control and data processing, Statistical modeling (Linear models, Multivariate models, Generalized and mixed models, Machine learning)
  * *Shinyapps*: For more interactive accessibility, our platform also develops custom R Shiny applications to make complex results easy to explore, share and reuse. We handle prototyping, development, secure deployment and maintenance, with a focus on reproducibility, documentation and sustainability.
  * *Functional Analysis*: Enrichment of metabolic or signalling pathways (GSEA), Transcriptional regulation (ISMARA, SCENIC), Intercellular communication (CellChat, NicheNet)
  * *Machine Learning*: Regularised regression, Random Forest, XGBoost gradient boosting, neural networks, Variable selection (glmnet,sPLS), Model validation (cross-validation, bootstrapping)
* **Training**: We train novice researchers and bioinformaticians in data analysis and statistics. We offer group courses, workshops, and coaching sessions. Through these activities, we introduce young researchers to new methodologies in computational biology and statistical modeling.
* **Tools**
  * [BrainTIME](https://joycelab.shinyapps.io/braintime/): The BrainTIME application allows users to explore gene expression within immune populations in the microenvironment of human brain tumours, with interactive filters and standardised comparisons.
  * [PAGEpy](https://github.com/sean-otoole/PAGEpy): Predictive Analysis of Gene Expression with Python
